About

Two small tools, built to actually be correct.

StudyMetrics is a unit converter and a GPA calculator — free, private, and built around getting the underlying math right rather than just looking right.

Why temperature gets special treatment

Most unit conversions are a simple multiplication — 1 mile is always 1.609344 kilometers, no matter the value. Temperature isn't: converting Celsius to Fahrenheit involves both a multiplication and an addition (°F = °C × 9/5 + 32), and it's a common bug in unit converters built by treating every category the same way. StudyMetrics handles temperature as its own dedicated conversion, verified against known fixed points — freezing, boiling, body temperature, and the -40° mark where both scales happen to agree.

How the GPA calculator handles edge cases

A weighted GPA is quality points divided by credit hours — not a simple average of your letter grades. A 4-credit course should count more than a 1-credit course. Pass/fail and audited courses (no letter grade, or zero credits) are automatically excluded rather than accidentally dragging your GPA in either direction, and the cumulative GPA blending uses the same quality-points math your registrar's office uses.

What we're honest about

The GPA calculator uses the standard US 4.0 scale, which is common but not universal — some schools weight AP or honors courses differently, or use a slightly different scale. For anything that needs to be official, check your school's registrar. The unit converter's data storage category uses 1024 as its base (matching how your computer reports file sizes), not the strict SI 1000-based standard — both conventions exist, and we picked the one most people expect.
