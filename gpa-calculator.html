Frequently asked questions

How is GPA calculated?

Each letter grade converts to grade points (A = 4.0 down to F = 0.0). Multiply each course's grade points by its credit hours, add those up across all courses, then divide by total credit hours. It's a credit-weighted average, not a simple average of your grades — a 4-credit A counts more than a 1-credit A.

What happens to pass/fail or audited courses?

Leave the grade blank or set credits to 0 for a course you don't want counted, and it's automatically excluded from the calculation — consistent with how pass/fail and audited courses are typically excluded from a standard GPA.

How does the cumulative GPA blending work?

Your previous GPA and previous total credits are converted back into total quality points (GPA × credits), combined with this semester's quality points and credits, then divided to get your new overall cumulative GPA — the same math your registrar uses.
